## Industry Fit Criteria

### Tier 1: Tech/Software (Score: 10)

**Why they're ideal**:
- AI adoption pressure creates urgency
- Technical teams need continuous upskilling
- High employee expectations for growth
- Budget typically available for L&D tech

**Key Pain Points**:
- Developer retention and growth paths
- AI/ML skill gaps across organization
- Remote team development challenges
- Skill obsolescence anxiety

**Signals to Look For**:
- Hiring AI/ML roles (skill gap indicator)
- "Digital transformation" in company news
- Remote-first or hybrid workforce
- Recent funding (budget available)

**Decision Makers**:
- VP Engineering
- CTO (if company <500)
- VP People / Chief People Officer
- Director of Engineering

---

### Tier 1: Healthcare/Biotech (Score: 10)

**Why they're ideal**:
- Regulatory compliance requires training
- Digital health transformation ongoing
- Chronic workforce shortages
- High stakes = high L&D investment

**Key Pain Points**:
- Compliance training at scale
- Digital health skills gap
- Clinical staff development
- Onboarding efficiency

**Signals to Look For**:
- New facility openings (hiring wave)
- Digital transformation announcements
- Regulatory changes affecting training
- Telehealth expansion

**Decision Makers**:
- Chief Learning Officer
- VP Human Resources
- Director of Clinical Education
- Chief People Officer

---

### Tier 1: Manufacturing/Industry 4.0 (Score: 10)

**Why they're ideal**:
- Automation creating massive reskilling need
- Blue-to-white collar transition underway
- Government incentives for training
- Clear ROI on skill development

**Key Pain Points**:
- Automation anxiety among workforce
- Digital skills for factory floor
- Succession planning for skilled trades
- Industry 4.0 capability building

**Signals to Look For**:
- Factory modernization investments
- Industry 4.0 / smart manufacturing mentions
- Automation technology purchases
- Skills training announcements

**Decision Makers**:
- VP Operations
- CHRO
- Director of Training
- Plant Manager (for site-specific pilots)

---

### Tier 1: Professional Services (Score: 9)

**Why they're ideal**:
- Knowledge work = continuous learning culture
- Competitive pressure for talent
- AI tools disrupting traditional work
- High employee education expectations

**Key Pain Points**:
- Training time vs billable hours conflict
- AI tool adoption across firm
- Talent retention and development
- Client-facing skill development

**Signals to Look For**:
- AI adoption announcements
- New practice area launches
- Partnership expansions
- Talent acquisition news

**Decision Makers**:
- Chief People Officer
- L&D Director
- Managing Partner (smaller firms)
- COO

---

### Tier 2: Other Industries (Score: 5-7)

**Consider if strong signals present**:
- Financial Services (7) - Compliance, digital transformation
- Retail (6) - Customer service training, digital skills
- Energy (6) - Transition to renewables, safety training
- Education (5) - Staff development, administrative skills

## Negative Filters (Disqualifiers)

**Immediate Disqualification**:
- Company size <150 employees
- Recent bankruptcy or severe financial distress
- Just signed multi-year LMS contract (last 6 months)
- Industry outside B2B (B2C retail, hospitality)

**Proceed with Caution**:
- Recent layoffs >10% workforce
- PE/VC portfolio company in turnaround mode
- No LinkedIn presence for decision makers
- Government/public sector (long procurement)

---

## Budget Indicators

**Strong Budget Signals**:
- Recent funding round (Series A+)
- Revenue growth >20% YoY
- Active job postings for L&D roles
- HR tech vendor partnerships announced
- Training budget mentioned in job posts

**Weak Budget Signals**:
- Hiring freeze announced
- Cost-cutting initiatives
- Leadership turnover in HR
- No L&D function visible

## Competitive Landscape

**Current Competitor Users** (displacement opportunity):
- LinkedIn Learning - High volume, low personalization
- Coursera for Business - Content-focused, not personalized
- Udemy Business - Similar positioning
- Workday Learning - Enterprise, expensive

**Position Skillia as**:
- More personalized than generic platforms
- AI-powered career guidance (unique)
- More affordable than enterprise solutions
- Faster ROI through pilot model
